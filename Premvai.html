About & Early Life

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi was born on 2 October 1869 in Porbandar, Gujarat. He went to London to study law and later practiced in South Africa where he developed many of his political ideas and methods. Gandhi returned to India and led mass movements for independence using non-violent civil disobedience.

Personal Lifestyle

Gandhi lived extremely simply: minimal possessions, plain khadi clothes he spun himself, and a strict daily routine of prayer, reading spiritual texts, and physical work. He practiced fasting both as a spiritual discipline and as a method of political protest.

Spinning (Charkha): Symbol of self-reliance and village industry; Gandhi spun thread daily.
Fasting: Used for self-purification and political persuasion; often fasted to influence public and followers.
Diet & Health: Vegetarian diet, experimented with simple foods and sometimes long fasts to test resolve and health.

Spiritual & Ethical Beliefs

Gandhi’s thought combined ideas from Hinduism, Jainism, Christianity and other traditions. He emphasised truth, non-violence, tolerance, and self-discipline. His moral practice was as important as his political strategy — he tried to live what he preached.

Short summary: simplicity, self-discipline, service to others.
